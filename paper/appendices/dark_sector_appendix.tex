\section{Meta--mystery and dark-sector elimination}
\label{app:dark-sector}

\begin{fixedbox}
\textbf{Consequence-facing only.} This appendix is an eliminative constraint profile for \metamysterydoc{}. It depends on the descriptive-closure and reference-stability results of Sections~7--9 together with the meta-necessity appendix immediately preceding it.
\end{fixedbox}

\subsection*{Fixed-domain object}
The fixed-domain object is any persistent ``mystery'' generated by a framework whose alleged core referent fails invariance under standing-preserving redescription, together with any dark-sector posit introduced to stabilize the resulting bookkeeping imbalance.

\begin{proposition}[Meta--mystery consequence profile]
Persistent deep mysteries are a diagnostic of upstream admissibility failure: if a framework repeatedly generates explanatory pressure around a putative entity whose referent is not invariant under standing-preserving redescription, then the mystery is not ontological depth but bookkeeping instability. Dark-sector posits introduced to absorb the residuals of such instability are therefore non-ontic unless they independently acquire redescription-invariant reference.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
This is the exact eliminative principle stated in \metamysterydoc{}. The source proves a meta--mystery theorem according to which persistent mysteries signal non-invariant core referents, then defines dark-sector posits as auxiliary structures introduced to absorb residuals, stabilize parameters, patch closure, or launder scope. Its theorem and corollaries show that patch cascades and dark-sector repairs are bookkeeping artifacts rather than ontology unless a redescription-invariant referent is fixed independently. The present appendix imports only that consequence-facing diagnostic.\cite{MaleyMetaMystery}
\end{proof}

\begin{reviewbox}
\textbf{Exact non-claims.} This appendix does not deny empirical adequacy of locally useful representational regimes, and it does not claim that every difficult scientific problem is ill-posed. Its narrower claim is that persistent mystery plus patch-cascade behavior is evidence of non-invariant reference, not of hidden ontological depth.\cite{MaleyMetaMystery}
\end{reviewbox}
